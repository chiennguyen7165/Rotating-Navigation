Overview of Ninh Binh province

Ninh Binh has abundant tourism resources with many historical, cultural relics, famous landscapes and valuable natural ecological environment. The terrain is diverse and distributed in three relatively pronounced areas, which fully bear the nuances of miniature Vietnam: high mountains, plains and coastal areas. With 3/4 of the area of ​​hilly, diverse karst terrain, rich flora and fauna has formed many resorts with beautiful natural landscapes, attracting tourists, Ninh Binh is famous for Tam Coc - Bich Dong, Cuc Phuong National Park, Van Long wetland nature reserve, Kim Son - Con Noi sea; lake systems as: Dong Chuong, Yen Thang, Dong Thai,etc

Ninh Binh ancient capital attracts visitors not only by the nostalgic beauty fascinating the hearts of people, but also by the famous specialties that attract domestic and foreign tourists. The specialties of this land have stirred the hearts of the visitors. Whenever going away or visiting, we want to bring unique gifts to send the unique features of the ancient capital as gifts to relatives and friends

Trang An Landscape Complex

Located within Ninh Binh Province of North Vietnam near the southern margin of the Red River Delta, the Trang An Landscape Complex (Trang An) is a mixed cultural and natural property contained mostly within three protected areas; the Hoa Lu Ancient Capital, the Trang An-Tam Coc-Bich Dong Scenic Landscape, and the Hoa Lu Special-Use Forest. The property covers 6,226 hectares within the Trang An limestone massif, and is surrounded by a buffer zone of 6,026 hectares, mostly rural land with rice paddy fields. There are about 14,000 residents, the majority of whom are families involved in subsistence agriculture, but much of the property is uninhabited and in a natural state. In particular, at 25th June, 2014, Trang An Landscape Complex was listed by UNESCO as a World Cultural and Natural Heritage
